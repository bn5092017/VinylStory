Vinyl Junkie

Singles

I have a collection of 7 inch singles, some purchased because I loved the song, others because of the novelty value. One of my favourites is Cornershop’s ‘Brimful of Asha’. This achieved more success with the b-side remix by Norman Cook than the original version. It was my introduction to this band and I bought the album ‘When I was born for the 7th time’ because I enjoyed it so much. Two of my Teenage Fanclub singles are ‘What you do to me’ and ‘Radio’. I love the sleeves on these singles. Talking Heads ‘And she was’ I bought for the bargain price of 50p.

Unusual items

I am a sucker for limited editions, particularly coloured vinyl singles. Many of those I have only listened to once, if even once. For example, the lemon single ‘Squashy Lemon Squeezy’ by Pocket Size was bought because of the lemon yellow vinyl. I had to check which was the band and which was the song on the back of the sleeve and I have no idea how it goes. I bought this set of singles by the Beatles in coloured vinyl in the Virgin Megastore on Aston Quay. They are quite expensive imports at £3.49 each. I bought Fountains of Wayne ‘I want an alien for Christmas’ because I love Christmas songs, so I do know how this one goes. ‘Make Me Smile (Come Up and See Me)’ by Steve Harley and Cockney Rebel is a 7 inch meant for a jukebox I think. It has no label, the information is etched into the vinyl. Primal Scream’s ‘Echo Dek’ is an album on five 7 inches. I bought this because I like the band, but mainly for the novelty value.
